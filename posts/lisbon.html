Where It All Began: A Love Letter to Lisbon

June 28, 2026 • 6 min read

The first thing I noticed about Lisbon wasn't the color of the tiles or the smell of grilled sardines drifting out of a tasca doorway — it was the light. Late afternoon here has a way of turning the whole city the color of a pastel de nata, warm and a little burnt at the edges, and I remember standing at the top of the Elevador da Bica with my suitcase still in my hand, thinking: I haven't even found a place to sleep yet, and I already don't want to leave.

That was two years and eight countries ago. I'd told myself Lisbon was going to be a three-week stopover before "real" travel began — Southeast Asia, the Sahara, wherever the cheap flights pointed. Instead I ended up renting a tiny apartment in Alfama with a window that opened directly onto a neighbor's clothesline, and a landlady named Dona Fátima who left a custard tart on my doorstep the morning after I moved in, no note, no explanation, just a small warm box that smelled like cinnamon and felt like being welcomed somewhere.

"You don't choose Lisbon," an old shoemaker on Rua de São Miguel told me one evening, without looking up from his work. "It decides whether it's going to let you stay."

I've spent more nights than I can count on the miradouros — the city's scattered viewpoints — watching the Tagus River turn silver, then rose, then a deep, ink-blue nothing, while somewhere behind me a fado singer's voice cracked open the quiet of a side street. There's a particular kind of sadness in fado, the Portuguese call it saudade, a longing for something you haven't even lost yet. I didn't understand the word until I tried to imagine packing up and leaving this city for good, and found I couldn't finish the thought.

So I didn't leave. Lisbon became the place I return to between trips, the apartment with the clothesline view became home base, and Dona Fátima's custard tarts became the thing I miss most violently at 3am in a hostel somewhere on the other side of the world. Every story on this blog technically starts here — on a hill, in the fading gold light, with a suitcase I still hadn't unpacked and a city that, against every plan I'd made, quietly decided to keep me.
